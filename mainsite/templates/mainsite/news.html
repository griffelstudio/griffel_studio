AR/VR in education

Visuals rule the world. It is true that vision is the main channel of getting information for most people. AR/VR tools allow you to transmit information in a highly illustrative manner. Architecture and engineering students are able to see structural elements in detail. It is not necessary (though still desirable) to visit a construction site in person to see how things are done. And obviously it is much safer to learn how to run the site or operate heavy machinery when you are in an auditorium with a headset on. Emotions help to absorb information.Traditional education ignores emotions as an important aspect of the teaching process. It is believed that emotions interfere with the information perception rather than help. However, we are convinced that any at first glance boring material can be learned more effectively when the student is involved in the process emotionally. AR/VR technologies can increase student involvement significantly.
